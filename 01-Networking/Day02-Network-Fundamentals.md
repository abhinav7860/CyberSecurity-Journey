# Day 2 - Mobile Connectivity

Today I studied how mobile devices connect to the internet and other devices using different wireless technologies.

---

## Mobile Devices and Wi-Fi

Nowadays, mobile devices let us study, work, communicate, and browse the internet from almost anywhere.

Almost every smartphone supports Wi-Fi.

### Why use Wi-Fi?
- Saves mobile data.
- Uses less battery than mobile data.
- Usually gives better internet speed.

### Some safety tips
- Don't enter passwords on unsecured websites.
- Use a VPN when sending important information.
- Secure your home Wi-Fi.
- Use WPA2 or WPA3 security.

---

## Wi-Fi Settings

Both Android and iPhone have Wi-Fi settings.

To connect:
- Turn on Wi-Fi.
- Choose the network.
- Enter the password.

If I move away from the Wi-Fi range, the phone automatically switches to mobile data.

If I come back into range, it reconnects automatically.

---

## Manual Wi-Fi Connection

Sometimes a Wi-Fi network won't show up.

Possible reasons:
- SSID is hidden.
- Auto-connect is disabled.

In that case, I have to enter:
- SSID (Wi-Fi name)
- Security type
- Password

If any of these are wrong, the connection won't work.

---

## Cellular Data

When Wi-Fi isn't available, the phone uses cellular data provided by the mobile network.

Different providers offer different speeds and data limits.

Most phones automatically switch between Wi-Fi and mobile data whenever needed.

---

## GPS (Global Positioning System)

GPS uses satellites to find the location of a phone.

It can usually find the location within about **10 meters**.

### Uses
- Google Maps
- Navigation
- Location sharing
- Tracking

---

## Bluetooth

Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology.

It is mainly used to connect nearby devices without cables.

Examples:
- Headphones
- Speakers
- Smartwatch
- Keyboard
- Mouse
- Car audio

Bluetooth uses very little power and can transfer both voice and data.

---

## Bluetooth Pairing

Before two Bluetooth devices can communicate, they need to be paired.

Steps:
- Turn on Bluetooth.
- Make the device discoverable.
- Scan for nearby devices.
- Select the device.
- Enter the PIN if required.

Once paired, the devices usually reconnect automatically.

---

## NFC (Near Field Communication)

NFC works only when two devices are very close to each other.

### Uses
- Contactless payments
- Smart cards
- Device pairing
- Sharing small files
