# Day 1 - Network Fundamentals

The Internet is a worldwide collection of interconnected networks. No one person or group owns it.

---

# 1. Network Types

### a. Small Home Networks
- Small home networks connect a few computers to each other and to the Internet.

### b. Small Office and Home Office (SOHO) Networks
- SOHO networks allow computers in a home office or a remote office to connect to a corporate network.

### c. Medium to Large Networks
- Used by corporations and schools.
- Can have many locations with hundreds or thousands of interconnected hosts.

### d. World Wide Networks
- The Internet is a network of networks that connects hundreds of millions of computers worldwide.

### Mobile Devices Used with the Internet
- Smartphone
- Tablet
- Smart glasses
- Other smart devices

---

# 2. Data Transmission

All of our data is stored and transmitted as a series of bits.

Each bit can only have one of two values:
- `0`
- `1`

Every input device helps translate human interactions into binary code so that the computer can understand them.

Every output device converts binary data into a human-readable form.

## Types of Personal Data

### a. Volunteered Data
Information explicitly shared by individuals, such as social network profiles.

### b. Observed Data
Information captured by recording user actions, such as location data from cell phones.

### c. Inferred Data
Information generated by analyzing volunteered or observed data, such as a credit score.

## Three Common Methods of Signal Transmission Used in Networks

### a. Electrical Signals
Transmission is achieved by representing data as electrical pulses on copper wire.

### b. Optical Signals
Transmission is achieved by converting electrical signals into light pulses.

### c. Wireless Signals
Transmission is achieved by using infrared, microwave, or radio waves through the air.

---

# 3. Bandwidth

Bandwidth is the maximum amount of data that can be transmitted over a network in a given amount of time.

### Units
- bps
- Kbps
- Mbps
- Gbps

Higher bandwidth means more data can be sent.

---

# 4. Throughput

Throughput is the actual amount of data successfully transferred.

Many factors influence throughput, including:

### a.
The amount of data being sent and received over the connection.

### b.
The types of data being transmitted.

### c.
The latency created by the number of network devices encountered between the source and destination.

---

# Example

### Bandwidth = Number of lanes on a highway

- 8-lane highway → High bandwidth
- 2-lane highway → Low bandwidth

### Throughput = Number of cars actually moving

Even on an 8-lane highway, traffic jams can reduce the number of cars passing.

This is similar to computer networks, where the actual data transferred (throughput) is often less than the maximum capacity (bandwidth).
